The various types of graphs

There are several types of charts used in technical and financial analysis to represent market data and price movements over time. Each of these charts offers a unique perspective on the past and present price behavior and can help traders make informed trading decisions. Some of the most common chart types include:

Bar-chart

Bar Chart:this type of chart represents the opening, closing, high and low price of a financial asset over a given time interval. Each vertical bar represents the range between the high and low prices, while the horizontal lines on the sides of the bar indicate the opening price (left) and the closing price (right).

Japanese candlestick

Japanese candlestick chart: this type of chart shows the opening, closing, high and low price of a financial asset over a specific time period. The "candles" are made up of a rectangular body (which represents the difference between the opening and closing price) and two "shadows" or "mechas" which represent the interval between the maximum and minimum price. The colors of the candles may vary depending on whether the closing price is higher or lower than the opening price.

Line Chart

Line Chart: this is the simplest type of chart, which represents the closing prices of a financial asset over a specific time period with the use of lines. This chart is useful for identifying long-term trends and general patterns in price behavior.
