What are patterns in trading

>In trading, the term "pattern" refers to specific configurations or formations that appear on the price charts of financial assets, such as stocks, currencies or commodities. These patterns are used by technical analysts to try to predict future price movements. Some common examples of patterns in trading include:

Head and Shoulders

Head and Shoulders: A pattern indicating a possible trend reversal, with a central peak surrounded by two smaller peaks

Triangle

Ascending or Descending Triangle: Indicates a possible imminent price breakout, with a series of increasing or decreasing highs or lows.

Flag

Flags and Pennants: These are continuation patterns that form after a strong price movement, indicating a temporary pause before a possible continuation of the trend
