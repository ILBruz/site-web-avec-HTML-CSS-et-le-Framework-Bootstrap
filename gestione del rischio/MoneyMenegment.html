Risk Manegement

Money management in trading refers to the practice of carefully managing invested capital in order to preserve the capital itself and minimize the risk of significant losses. It is a fundamental component of the trading strategy that aims to preserve capital in periods of losses and maximize profits in periods of profit. Effective money management can help investors achieve their long-term financial goals and responsibly manage the risk associated with trading. Here are some key principles of money management in trading:

Rule n°1

Determine position size: involves allocating a fixed percentage of total capital to each trade. This can help you control overall risk and avoid investing too much capital in a single trade..

Rule n°2

Set stop losses and take profits: stop losses determine the point at which a trade will be closed to limit losses, while take profits determine the point at which a trade will be closed to guarantee a certain level of profit. These levels are determined based on the investor's trading strategy and risk tolerance.

Rule n°3

Risk/Reward Ratio: this ratio indicates the relationship between the risk taken on a position and the potential gain. Traders seek to maintain an acceptable balance between risk and return, seeking to maximize profits without exposing themselves to excessive risk.
